My Journey

The Late Beginning

  The, albeit later in life, pursuit of an engineering degree for me, actually started with Electrical Engineering. Working as a calibration technician in a Metrology department (see Metrology?), with my focus being mainly electrical calibrations, it seemed like the best fit for where I was. Once I started taking primary focus classes, it became quickly apparent that I did not enjoy the content offered, and would likely not enjoy the field either.

My pace had slowed, I was taking one class at a time, even at seven-and-a-half weeks per class, that is only two a semester, it was going to be quite some time till I would be done, even being just a bit half-way through the EE program. I had an assembly language programming course that needed to be done, around the time I enrolled for the course, I discovered ASU offered an on-line Software Engineering degree program. I did not know it then, but my path was going to change.

The Transition

  The realization that there was enjoyment to be had, for myself, in the type of content being offered in the assembly language course was more than I expected. The sort of problem solving needed felt more natural. The fit for the content and challenges felt appropriate. I requested to transition to the Software Engineering program before the course was finished.

The next step, take the first programming course required, and see if the appropriateness stuck. Why would I continue with this path if I did not enjoy it?

The Sojourn

  First class in the program, Object-Oriented programming, since the degree path was Java focused, this was also my first Java class. I did really well, well enough that I was helping others in my class with issues they encountered. This experience opened my eyes a bit, I felt like I was doing something I could be good at, if felt like it could be, or was, the best fit for where I wanted to be.

So far the course work has been great, and I'm flying through it faster than I ever was with EE. It is currently 2019 as of this writing, and the finish line is Spring 2020!

The Next Phase

  So the inevitable, what is next? This is still uncertain. I'm a thinker, sometimes to my detriment (or my wife's), and decisions like this require lots of time for me to process. There are lots of opportunities out there for my skill set I feel, and not just strictly what I have to offer from my degree, but soft skills as well. At least I have a better idea for where I want to be than when I started my journey.

I would love to stay with my current employer, since they have made my education journey possible. Who knows what is on the horizon, I know I don't, so I'll keep scouting.
